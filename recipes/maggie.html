SPICY MAGGIE RECIPE

HOME

Maggi noodles are one of the popular instant comfort foods that have reached a staple place in kitchens worldwide, particularly in India. Packed with its fast cooking time and characteristic masala taste, Maggi noodles are ideal for hectic days, late-night meals, or an evening snack before meals. The recipe takes the traditional Maggi experience and throws in a hint of creativity using optional vegetables and spices to supplement nutrition and flavor.


Easy to prepare and customizable, this version of Maggi can be made in under 10 minutes and tailored to your taste. Soupy or dry, spicy or full of veggies, this dish is one that encourages experimentation while still giving you the nostalgia everyone craves.

INGREDIENTS

  • ¼ tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • ½ onion, finely chopped
  • ¼ capsicum, finely chopped
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 pinch chaat masala (optional, for tanginess)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • Maggi tastemaker (from the pack)

How to make Spicy maggie

  1. Heat oil in a pan on medium flame. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Sauté green chilies and onions until translucent.
  3. Add chopped capsicum and tomatoes. Cook for 2–3 minutes until soft.
  4. Add turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and chaat masala (if using). Mix well.
  5. Pour in 1½ cups water and bring it to a boil.
  6. Add Maggi noodles and the tastemaker masala from the packet. Stir to combine.
  7. Cook uncovered for 2–3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the noodles soften and absorb the flavors.
  8. Once done, turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.


Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich, creamy texture and bold, savory flavor—all achieved without using any cream. Originating from Rome, this traditional recipe combines just a few simple ingredients: eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta (or guanciale), and freshly ground black pepper. The result is a comforting and elegant dish that captures the essence of Italian cooking—simplicity, balance, and quality.


Perfect for both a quick weeknight dinner and an impressive meal for guests, Carbonara relies on technique rather than complexity. The heat of the freshly cooked pasta gently cooks the eggs, creating a luscious sauce that clings to each strand of spaghetti. With each bite, you'll enjoy the salty richness of the pancetta, the sharpness of the cheese, and the subtle warmth of black pepper—an unforgettable combination that's stood the test of time.

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta (or bacon), diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup grated Pecorino Romano (or Parmesan)
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

How to make Spaghetti Carbonara

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook until crispy. If using garlic, sauté it with the pancetta and remove it once browned.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Turn off the heat under the pancetta pan. Add the hot, drained spaghetti to the pan and toss well to coat it with the pancetta and its fat.
  5. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta and quickly toss everything together. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce.
  6. Add a bit of the reserved pasta water, if needed, to loosen the sauce to your desired consistency.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.


Tandoori Paneer Momos Recipe

Tandoori Paneer Momos are a delicious fusion of Indo-Tibetan dumplings and North Indian tandoori flavors. These momos are filled with a spicy, flavorful mixture of crumbled paneer (Indian cottage cheese), vegetables, and aromatic spices, then steamed or pan-fried and finally coated with a smoky tandoori marinade. The result is a vibrant, tangy, and slightly charred treat that packs a punch with every bite.


Perfect as a starter or evening snack, Tandoori Paneer Momos are a crowd favorite at street food stalls and parties alike. Served hot with mint chutney or spicy red sauce, they offer a delightful combination of soft, juicy filling and a crisp, slightly charred outer layer. Whether cooked in a tandoor, oven, or grill pan, this dish brings the essence of tandoori cuisine to the much-loved momo.


INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for kneading)
  • 200g paneer (crumbled)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup thick curd (yogurt)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (Kashmiri preferred for color)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil or melted butter

How to make Tandoori Paneer Momos

  1. Mix flour, salt, and water to form a soft dough. Cover and rest for 20 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, mix crumbled paneer, onion, green chili, garam masala, and salt for the filling.
  3. Roll out small dough balls into thin discs, fill with the paneer mixture, and shape into momos.
  4. Steam the momos for 10–12 minutes until cooked.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix curd, red chili powder, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and oil to make the marinade.
  6. Coat the steamed momos in the marinade and rest for 10 minutes.
  7. Grill or pan-fry until slightly charred and golden.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or sauce.
